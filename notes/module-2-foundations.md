## 📌 1. Machine Learning vs. Deep Learning vs. Foundation Models

* **Machine Learning (ML):** Statistical algorithms that learn patterns from data. Includes Supervised, Unsupervised, and Reinforcement learning.
* **Deep Learning (DL):** A subset of ML using multi-layered **Neural Networks** to solve complex problems like image recognition.
* **Foundation Models:** Large-scale models (like LLMs) trained on vast amounts of data that can be adapted to a wide range of downstream tasks.

---

## 🏗️ 2. Core AI Architectures
* **Cognitive Computing:** Systems that simulate human thought processes (reasoning, learning) to assist in decision-making.
* **Neural Networks:** Computational models inspired by the human brain's biological neurons, consisting of Input, Hidden, and Output layers.
* **Large Language Models (LLMs):** Massive neural networks specialized in processing and generating human-like text based on probability and context.

## 💡 Summary of Foundations
The transition from **Discriminative AI** to **Generative AI** relies on Foundation Models. While traditional AI classifies (e.g., "Is this a car?"), GenAI creates (e.g., "Generate a 3D model of a car").
